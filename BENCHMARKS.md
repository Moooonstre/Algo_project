# ASNAP - Performance benchmarks

Generated by `python benchmark.py` (standard library `time` only). Times in
milliseconds. Exact methods solve the NP-Hard problems optimally but their cost
grows fast with N; the greedy / DP heuristics stay cheap. This is the
"analyze the efficiency of your algorithms" evidence required by the project
(section 3) and the scaling questions of LAB 9 / LAB 10.

## Dominating set - exact (brute force) vs greedy

| N  | exact (ms) | exact size | greedy (ms) | greedy size |
|----|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 5  | 0.02      | 2         | 0.013      | 2           |
| 10 | 0.08      | 3         | 0.021      | 3           |
| 15 | 0.24      | 3         | 0.031      | 3           |
| 18 | 0.83      | 3         | 0.049      | 3           |

Exact time roughly grows with the 2^N subsets explored; greedy stays flat and
matched the optimal size on these instances.

## Maximum independent set - exact (backtracking) vs greedy

| N  | exact (ms) | exact size | greedy (ms) | greedy size |
|----|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 5  | 0.02      | 3         | 0.022      | 3           |
| 10 | 0.08      | 3         | 0.023      | 3           |
| 15 | 0.30      | 6         | 0.054      | 6           |
| 20 | 0.65      | 8         | 0.080      | 8           |
| 25 | 2.41      | 8         | 0.095      | 7           |

At N=25 the greedy returns 7 vs the optimal 8 - the expected quality/speed
trade-off (greedy is ~25x faster but can miss the optimum).

## 0/1 Knapsack - exact DP vs greedy ratio

| N   | budget | DP (ms) | DP value | greedy (ms) | greedy value |
|-----|--------|---------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| 50  | 100    | 0.54    | 1464     | 0.025       | 1461         |
| 100 | 500    | 5.37    | 3721     | 0.038       | 3710         |
| 200 | 1000   | 22.40   | 7191     | 0.070       | 7191         |
| 300 | 2000   | 71.26   | 13201    | 0.125       | 13189        |

DP time grows as O(N x budget) (pseudo-polynomial); the greedy is near-optimal
and orders of magnitude faster, illustrating why large-scale platforms favour
heuristics.
